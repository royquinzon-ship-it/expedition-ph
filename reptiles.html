The Collection

Philippine Reptiles

Twenty-four species — crocodilians, sea turtles, pythons, lizards, geckos, cobras, and frogs — a cross-section of the cold-blooded life of the Philippine archipelago.

Reptile · Crocodilian
Philippine CrocodileCrocodylus mindorensis

One of the world's most endangered crocodilians, found only in freshwater habitats of the Philippines. Estimated fewer than 250 individuals remain.

Critically Endangered
Reptile · Snake
Philippine CobraNaja philippinensis

One of the world's most venomous cobras — capable of spitting venom with deadly accuracy. Endemic to the northern Philippines, it inhabits lowland forests, grasslands, and rice paddies.

Least Concern
Reptile · Viper
Philippine Pit ViperTrimeresurus flavomaculatus

A venomous tree-dwelling viper found across the Philippine archipelago. Its heat-sensing pits make it a precise nocturnal predator of the forest undergrowth and canopy.

Vulnerable
Reptile · Lizard
Philippine Sailfin LizardHydrosaurus pustulatus

A striking semi-aquatic lizard found along Philippine riverbanks and coasts. Named for the dramatic sail-like crest on its tail, it can run on water and is an excellent swimmer.

Vulnerable
Reptile · Turtle
Philippine Forest TurtleSiebenrockiella leytensis

One of the world's most endangered freshwater turtles — found only in Leyte and Palawan. Critically threatened by habitat loss and collection for the illegal pet trade.

Critically Endangered
Reptile · Snake
Samar CobraNaja samarensis

A venomous cobra endemic to the southern Philippines — found on Mindanao, Samar, Leyte, and nearby islands. Its cytotoxic venom and bold temperament make it one of the most medically significant snakes in the region.

Least Concern
Reptile · Monitor
Gray's MonitorVaranus olivaceus

The world's only fruit-eating monitor lizard — a remarkable evolutionary anomaly. Found on Luzon and nearby islands, it climbs trees to reach fruiting canopy plants and plays an irreplaceable role as a forest seed disperser.

Vulnerable
Reptile · Monitor
Ornate MonitorVaranus ornatus

A large, powerfully built monitor lizard found across the Philippine archipelago. An opportunistic predator, it hunts fish, crabs, birds, and small mammals across a wide range of coastal and forest habitats.

Vulnerable
Reptile · Lizard
Philippine Flying DragonDraco volans

A small agamid lizard capable of gliding between trees by extending its elongated ribs to form a colourful wing membrane. Found across Philippine lowland and montane forests, it is one of the most acrobatic reptiles in Southeast Asia — and a common sight on the trunks of large forest trees.

Least Concern
Reptile · Gecko
Tokay GeckoGekko gecko

The largest gecko in the Philippines — its loud two-syllable call 'to-kay' echoes through Philippine homes and forests at night. A voracious predator of insects and small vertebrates, considered a symbol of good luck but heavily targeted by the illegal wildlife trade for supposed medicinal properties.

Least Concern
Reptile · Skink
Philippine Sun SkinkEutropis multicarinata

One of the most widespread lizards in the Philippines — its metallic bronze-and-copper scales glimmer in forest sunlight. Found from sea level to over 1,500 metres, this adaptable skink occupies gardens, farms, and primary forest alike, making it one of the most commonly encountered reptiles across the archipelago.

Least Concern
Reptile · Sea Turtle
Green Sea TurtleChelonia mydas

One of five sea turtle species nesting on Philippine beaches. The Philippines hosts critical nesting sites in the Turtle Islands, Palawan, and Batanes — and territorial waters serve as important feeding grounds for this ancient, legally protected ocean traveller.

Endangered
Reptile · Sea Turtle
Hawksbill Sea TurtleEretmochelys imbricata

Critically endangered and among the most threatened sea turtles in the world. Philippine reefs are critical foraging and nesting habitat — yet its striking shell has made it the most heavily exploited sea turtle in history, decimated by centuries of trade in tortoiseshell.

Critically Endangered
Reptile · Snake
King CobraOphiophagus hannah

The world's longest venomous snake — found in the forests of Luzon, Palawan, Mindanao, and the Visayas. Despite its intimidating size, it preys almost exclusively on other snakes and is rarely aggressive unless threatened or cornered near a nest.

Vulnerable
Reptile · Snake
Reticulated PythonMalayopython reticulatus

The world's longest snake — found in the rainforests of Luzon, Mindanao, and Palawan. This powerful constrictor is an apex predator capable of taking deer and large pigs. Despite its size, it is increasingly threatened by habitat loss and hunting for skin and meat.

Least Concern
Reptile · Snake
Blue-lipped Sea KraitLaticauda laticaudata

A venomous amphibious sea snake found on Philippine coral reefs and rocky shorelines. Banded in black and blue-grey, it hunts eels and small fish underwater before hauling out onto rocks to rest and digest. Despite its potent venom, it is remarkably docile and rarely bites unprovoked.

Least Concern
Reptile · Monitor
Water MonitorVaranus salvator

The Philippines' largest lizard — reaching up to 2.5 metres. An adaptable apex predator found from mangrove coasts to highland rivers, it swims powerfully and scavenges alongside crocodiles in Philippine wetlands. Locally known as Bayawak, it plays a vital ecological role as a scavenger and predator.

Least Concern
Reptile · Lizard
Philippine Forest DragonGonocephalus sophiae

A spectacular crested agamid lizard found in the highland forests of Palawan and Mindanao. Its rough, bark-like colouring makes it nearly impossible to spot when motionless on a mossy branch — yet when displaying, the male's vivid dewlap blazes like a flame in the forest understorey.

Least Concern
Reptile · Turtle
Philippine Soft-shell TurtlePelochelys cantorii

One of the world's largest freshwater turtles — found in the major river systems of Luzon, Mindanao, and Palawan. Its leathery, unmineralised shell distinguishes it from all other Philippine turtles. Critically threatened by hunting, sand mining, and the loss of the large river habitats it depends on.

Endangered
Amphibian · Frog
Philippine Giant Forest FrogLimnonectes leytensis

One of the largest frogs in the Philippines — a powerful, fanged predator of forest streams. Found across Leyte, Samar, and Mindanao, it hunts crabs, smaller frogs, and invertebrates from streamside rocks, defending territories with surprisingly aggressive physical combat.

Vulnerable
Amphibian · Frog
Mindanao Horned Forest FrogCeratobatrachus guentheri

A remarkable frog of Mindanao forest floors whose eggs hatch directly as miniature froglets, with no tadpole stage at all. A master of leaf-litter camouflage, it ambushes invertebrates from the forest floor and is rarely detected despite being widespread across the southern Philippines.

Least Concern
Amphibian · Frog
Philippine Flying FrogRhacophorus pardalis

A spectacular gliding frog found in the rainforests of Palawan and northern Mindanao. Its fully webbed feet act as parachutes, allowing glides of over 12 metres between trees. It lays eggs in foam nests above water — the tadpoles drop into the water below when they hatch.

Least Concern
Reptile · Snake
Philippine Rat SnakePtyas luzonensis

A large, fast-moving, non-venomous snake endemic to Luzon. A skilled climber and agile predator of rodents, birds, and lizards, it plays a critical role in controlling rat populations in Philippine agricultural landscapes — making it one of the most ecologically valuable snakes in the archipelago.

Least Concern
Amphibian · Frog
Palawan Horned FrogMegophrys ligayae

A master of camouflage — its textured skin and leaf-like shape make it nearly indistinguishable from the forest floor. Endemic to Palawan's lowland and montane rainforests.

Vulnerable

The Stakes of Survival

The Philippines has lost over 70% of its original forest cover. Every species documented here depends on urgent collective action.
