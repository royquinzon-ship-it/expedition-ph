Philippine Eagle
Pithecophaga jefferyi

The national bird of the Philippines and one of the world's rarest, largest, and most powerful birds of prey. Found only in the rainforests of Mindanao, Leyte, Samar, and Luzon, this magnificent raptor reigns at the top of the forest food chain. Its wingspan can reach up to 2 metres, and it preys on monkeys, flying lemurs, and large bats. Fewer than 800 individuals remain in the wild.

The Collection

Philippine Birds

Twenty-four iconic species from 706 recorded — raptors, hornbills, parrots, doves, pittas, kingfishers, and songbirds found nowhere else on Earth.

Bird · Raptor
Philippine EaglePithecophaga jefferyi

The world's most powerful raptor. Wingspan up to 2m. Found only in Philippine rainforests. National bird and symbol of conservation.

Critically Endangered
Bird · Parrot
Philippine CockatooCacatua haematuropygia

Known as the Katala — once widespread across the Philippines, now surviving mainly on Palawan. Community-led conservation has helped stabilise populations.

Critically Endangered
Bird · Hornbill
Philippine HornbillBuceros hydrocorax

The Rufous Hornbill, known locally as Kalaw, is a forest sentinel found across the Philippines. Its loud call echoes through lowland and montane rainforests.

Vulnerable
Bird · Pheasant
Palawan Peacock-PheasantPolyplectron napoleonis

Among the most stunning birds of the Philippine forest floor — the male's metallic blue-green plumage and iridescent eyespots make it one of the most beautiful pheasants in the world. Endemic to Palawan.

Vulnerable
Bird · Dove
Luzon Bleeding-Heart DoveGallicolumba luzonica

One of the most striking ground doves in the world — its vivid crimson breast patch mimics a bleeding wound. Found in the forests of Luzon and nearby islands, it walks the forest floor in search of seeds and invertebrates.

Vulnerable
Bird · Dove
Mindoro Bleeding-Heart DoveGallicolumba platenae

One of the rarest and most mysterious doves in the world — confined to the forests of Mindoro. Its secretive ground-dwelling habits make it nearly impossible to study, and almost nothing is known of its behaviour.

Critically Endangered
Bird · Owl
Philippine Eagle-OwlBubo philippensis

One of the largest owls in the world — a powerful nocturnal predator of Philippine lowland and foothill forests. Its deep booming call is one of the most haunting sounds of the Philippine night.

Vulnerable
Bird · Hornbill
Walden's HornbillRhabdotorrhinus waldeni

Found only on Panay and Negros — one of the world's most critically endangered hornbills. Deforestation has reduced its population to the point where individual nesting pairs are tracked by conservationists.

Critically Endangered
Bird · Waterfowl
Philippine Forest DuckAnas luzonica

The only dabbling duck endemic to the Philippines. Found across Luzon, Mindanao, and surrounding islands in rivers, lakes, and mangroves. Its chestnut and white plumage is unmistakable among Philippine waterbirds.

Vulnerable
Bird · Leafbird
Philippine LeafbirdChloropsis flavipennis

A jewel of the Visayan forest — the male's iridescent green and blue plumage is among the most spectacular of any Philippine songbird. Found on Cebu, Negros, and Leyte, it is a vital pollinator and seed disperser whose survival depends on the last remaining lowland forest fragments of the Visayas.

Endangered
Bird · Raptor
Philippine Serpent EagleSpilornis holospilus

A medium-sized raptor found across the Philippine archipelago. A specialist predator of snakes and lizards — its piercing yellow eyes and loud, distinctive call make it a recognisable presence in Philippine forests.

Least Concern
Bird · Hornbill
Sulu HornbillAnthracoceros montani

One of the world's most critically endangered hornbills — found only on Tawi-Tawi in the Sulu Archipelago. With fewer than 40 individuals remaining, it faces imminent extinction without urgent intervention.

Critically Endangered
Bird · Parrot
Philippine Hanging ParrotLoriculus philippensis

A tiny, vivid green parrot known for sleeping upside down like a bat. Found across the Philippine archipelago, it feeds on nectar, pollen, and soft fruit — playing an important role as a forest pollinator.

Least Concern
Bird · Owl
Giant Scops OwlOtus gurneyi

One of the world's largest scops owls — believed extinct for over 80 years before its dramatic rediscovery in Mindanao. This massive, forest-dwelling owl is still rarely encountered and poorly understood.

Vulnerable
Bird · Sunbird
Flaming SunbirdAethopyga flagrans

A brilliantly coloured nectar-feeding bird of Philippine highland forests. The male's fiery red and orange plumage is among the most spectacular of any small Philippine bird, flashing like an ember through the forest canopy.

Least Concern
Bird · White-eye
Lowland White-eyeZosterops meyeni

A small, sociable bird endemic to the Philippine lowlands — recognisable by the distinctive white ring around its eye. Often the nucleus of large mixed-species feeding flocks, making it a keystone species of forest bird communities.

Least Concern
Bird · Raptor
Philippine FalconetMicrohierax erythrogenys

The world's smallest falcon — a pocket-sized predator that hunts from open perches across Philippine lowland forests and forest edges. Despite its tiny size, it is bold and aggressive, regularly taking prey larger than itself.

Least Concern
Bird · Raptor
Philippine Hawk-EagleNisaetus philippensis

A powerful, crested eagle endemic to the forests of Luzon and the Visayas. Second only to the Philippine Eagle among the archipelago's forest raptors — it hunts birds, lizards, and small mammals through the dense rainforest canopy with remarkable agility.

Vulnerable
Bird · Trogon
Philippine TrogonHarpactes ardens

One of the most colourful birds in the Philippine forest — the male's deep crimson breast and emerald-green back make it a striking sight perched motionless in the shaded understorey. Found across the major Philippine islands in primary and mature secondary forest.

Near Threatened
Bird · Pitta
Philippine PittaErythropitta erythrogaster

One of the most vividly coloured birds in the Philippine forest — its crimson, cobalt, and emerald plumage blazes on the forest floor. Found across the major Philippine islands, it hunts earthworms and invertebrates among leaf litter with a quick, jerky gait.

Least Concern
Bird · Parrot
Blue-naped ParrotTanygnathus lucionensis

A large, vivid green parrot with a distinctive turquoise-blue nape — found across the Philippine archipelago. A noisy, sociable bird of forest canopy and forest edges, it is heavily traded as a pet, reducing wild populations significantly across its range.

Near Threatened
Bird · Kingfisher
Philippine KingfisherCeyx melanurus

A tiny, fiery-orange endemic kingfisher found along the forest streams of Luzon. One of the most threatened kingfishers in the world — its dependence on clean, clear forest streams means it vanishes as soon as forest cover is removed upstream.

Endangered
Bird · Bulbul
Philippine BulbulHypsipetes philippinus

One of the most vocal and widespread forest birds of the Philippine archipelago. Its loud, rich calls are among the defining sounds of Philippine forest — and as a prolific fruit-eater and seed disperser, it plays a critical role in forest regeneration across multiple island groups.

Least Concern
Bird · Parrot
Johnstone's LorikeetTrichoglossus johnstoniae

A small, brilliantly coloured lorikeet found only in the montane forests of Mindanao above 1,000 metres. One of the least-known parrots in the Philippines — its high-altitude habitat and secretive habits make it rarely encountered even by experienced birders.

Vulnerable

Conservation

The Stakes of Survival

The Philippines has lost over 70% of its original forest cover. Every species documented here depends on urgent collective action.
