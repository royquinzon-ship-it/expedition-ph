Featured Hall
Hall I
The Living Reef

The Philippines harbors the world's most diverse coral reef ecosystems. Sitting at the apex of the Coral Triangle, these underwater gardens are home to over 500 coral species and thousands of reef fish. The collection documents reef morphology, symbiotic relationships, and the impact of warming oceans on one of Earth's most complex habitats.

Explore Marine Life →
Hall II
🌿
Philippine Flora

Over 9,253 vascular plant species call the Philippines home — more than 3,500 found nowhere else on Earth. Explore ancient dipterocarp forests, carnivorous pitcher plants of Palawan, the towering Molave, and endemic orchids of extraordinary rarity including the Waling-Waling, queen of Philippine orchids.

Explore Flora →
Hall III
🦅
Winged Archipelago

706 bird species call these islands home. Witness the Philippine Eagle — the world's largest and most powerful raptor — alongside 238 endemic species found nowhere else. From the lowland hornbills to the highland pittas, this hall celebrates the Philippines as one of the world's premier birdwatching destinations.

Explore Birds →
Hall IV
⛰️
Earth & Stone

The Philippine archipelago sits on the Pacific Ring of Fire, shaped by titanic geological forces over millions of years. Explore volcanic formations from Mayon, Pinatubo, and Taal, alongside ancient seabeds now lifted into mountain ranges. Discover the fossils that reveal the islands' deep past — including remains of ancient elephants and stegodonts.

Explore Geology →
Hall V
🦎
Hidden Fauna

From the world's smallest primate — the Philippine Tarsier — to the Palawan Bearcat and the Philippine Crocodile, discover the mammals, reptiles, and amphibians of an island paradise. This hall also features the Sailfin Lizard, the Butaan monitor, and a remarkable diversity of geckos and skinks unique to specific islands.

Explore Fauna →
Hall VI
💧
Freshwater Worlds

Ancient lakes like Lanao and Taal harbor unique endemic life forms shaped by millions of years of isolation. Lake Lanao alone contains over 17 endemic species of cyprinid fish found nowhere else on Earth. Witness evolution in microcosm — a living laboratory of adaptive radiation driven by geological isolation.
